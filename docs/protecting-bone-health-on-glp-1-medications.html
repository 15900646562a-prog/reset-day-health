How to Protect Your Bone Health While on GLP-1 Medications

By the Reset Day Health Education Team · General education, not medical advice

Are you currently taking GLP-1 medications like Mounjaro or Zepbound? While these treatments can aid in weight loss, they may also lead to rapid bone density loss, particularly for women over 40. Understanding the connection between these medications and your bone health is essential for maintaining overall wellness.

In this article, we’ll explore strategies to protect your bones while managing your weight and cravings. Plus, we’ll introduce T-Patch, the no-needle way to access tirzepatide, the same active ingredient found in GLP-1 medications.

Understanding GLP-1 Medications and Bone Health

GLP-1 medications work by mimicking the incretin hormones that your body naturally produces to regulate insulin and appetite. However, studies indicate that these medications can lead to rapid bone density loss due to their effects on metabolism. This is particularly concerning for individuals over 40, who may already be experiencing natural changes in bone density.

Strategies for Protecting Your Bones

  • Increase Protein Intake: Aim for at least 150 grams of protein daily to support muscle and bone health.
  • Vitamin D Supplementation: Consider taking 5,000 units of vitamin D to enhance calcium absorption and bone strength.
  • Include Magnesium: Magnesium plays a crucial role in bone formation and health.
  • Resistance Training: Engage in resistance training at least three times a week to stimulate bone density and maintain strength.

Recognizing the Importance of Bone Health

It’s vital to understand that losing weight on GLP-1 medications can also mean losing bone density. Taking proactive steps to protect your bones can help you avoid fractures and maintain a healthy lifestyle as you age. Always consult with your healthcare provider to create a tailored plan that suits your needs.
