Is your tirzepatide legit? How to actually check

The GLP-1 and peptide space exploded — and so did the number of sketchy sellers. A lot of "brands" are just a logo on top of overseas powder, with a lab report that may not even match what's in the bottle. If you're going to put something in your body, you should be able to verify it. Here's exactly what to check.

1. Real third-party lab testing + a COA you can actually see

This is the single biggest tell. A legitimate source tests each batch at an independent third-party lab and gives you a Certificate of Analysis (COA) you can read — identity, purity, and contaminant screening. Be skeptical of "tested" claims with no document. Reset Day publishes real third-party COAs you can check — purity you can verify, not just a marketing word.

2. Made in a real GMP facility — not repackaged overseas powder

Ask where it's actually manufactured. GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice) facilities follow documented quality controls. Many sellers dropship overseas bulk powder and never touch a regulated facility. Reset Day's T-Patch is made in a real GMP facility, with a traceable, auditable supply chain.

3. Made and shipped in the US — with a real company behind it

US-made and US-shipped means faster delivery and a clearer accountability trail — not three weeks from an unmarked overseas envelope. Look for a real company: traceable batches, real customer support, a contactable business — not a one-post influencer drop that disappears.

Your quick checklist

  • Can they show a real third-party COA for the batch?
  • Is it made in a GMP facility (not repackaged overseas powder)?
  • Where is it made and shipped from?
  • Is there a real, contactable company and support behind it?

If a seller can't answer these, that's your answer. Talk to your healthcare provider before starting any medication.
